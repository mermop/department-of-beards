Frequently Asked Questions

What if I can't afford to pay the Australian Beard Tax (ABT)?

If you earn under $35,000, you may be eligible for a shaving kit.

Enquire at Centrelink for a complimentary Beard Assistance Kit (BAK).

Are minors under the age of 18 required to pay the Australian Beard Tax (ABT)?

Yes, minors are required to pay the ABT.

Enquire at Centrelink for a Bearded Teenager Shaving Kit (BTSK).

Do I need to pay the Australian Beard Tax (ABT) if I only have a beard for part of the financial year?

Yes. However, a BeardCoin may be redeemed at registered barbers for a beard shaving service and/or partial refund.

I don't normally wear a beard, but I sometimes just don't shave for a while. Do I need to pay the Australian Beard Tax (ABT)?

Yes. Any beard over 5mm that either covers the chin or connects the hairline from left to right is covered by the legislation. Wearing a beard without a valid BeardCoin is an offense.

Are there religious exemptions for paying the Australian Beard Tax (ABT)?

Yes. If your primary reason for wearing a beard is religious in nature, you may be eligible for an exemption under section 6D of the Australian​ ​Beard​ ​Tax​ ​(Promotion​ ​of​ ​Enlightenment​ ​Values) Act​ ​1934.

Are visitors subject to the Australian Beard Tax (ABT)?

Bearded visitors to Australia who have not obtained a BeardCoin will be given a 14-day Notice to Remedy, during which they should either remove their beard or pay the Australian Beard Tax (ABT).
